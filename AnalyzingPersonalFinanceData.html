ASK

For this project we’ll be examining an individual’s personal spending habits to determine how to better utilize their income and expenses. Some questions and insights we can determine with this individual are:

  1. Identifying excessive/unnecessary spending habits
  2. Cutting out superfluous purchases
  3. Ensuring that every month, income will exceed expenses
  4. Strategies to maximize income/other income streams

PREPARE

We first clean up some data to confirm the integrity. For instance, at first glance it seems as though this person paid for their music streaming account every month for an entire year. We can confirm this by using a COUNTIF=(B:B, “Spotify) function to ensure that there are 12 transactions.

We then load the .csv into BigQuery to conduct some SQL queries. Specifically, we want to look for patterns by month:

  1. Spending Patterns
  2. Monthly income

We are also given a simple monthly budget to work with

PROCESS

  1. Firstly, we determine this person’s income with the following query:

SELECT

    SUM(Amount)

FROM

    FinanceProject.Budget

WHERE

    Description= 'Biweekly Paycheck'

We determine that this person has an annual income of $52,000 per year or $4,333.33 per month.

  1. Next, we’ll want to determine total monthly expenses:

SELECT 

    SUM(Amount) 

FROM

    FinanceProject.Budget

WHERE 

    description!='Biweekly Paycheck'

AND

    description!='Credit Card Payment' 

Returns to us a value of $35,443.54 per year or $2,953.63 per month

This gives us the simple averages of income and expenses per month, but we can delve deeper into the data and paint a better overall picture of spending trends.

  1. Tracking total expenses per month
  1. This should give us a better understanding of the months when this individual is spending the most/least.

We can use the following code in SQL to extract this data:

SELECT 

    EXTRACT(MONTH FROM Date), SUM(amount)

FROM 

    FinanceProject.Budget

WHERE 

    description!='Biweekly Paycheck'

AND

    description!='Credit Card Payment' 

GROUP BY 

    EXTRACT(MONTH FROM Date)

  1. Tracking expenses per category
  1. We can look at this as a total or monthly spending by category

SELECT 

    EXTRACT(MONTH FROM Date), SUM(amount)

FROM 

    FinanceProject.Budget

WHERE 

    Category='Alcohol & Bars'

GROUP BY 

    EXTRACT(MONTH FROM Date)

ORDER BY 

    max(Date)

All data was verified by confirming with Excel file.

ANALYSIS/CONCLUSIONS

As shown from our Tableau visualization, we can deduce the following conclusions:

  1. Income versus expenses is impressive, as this individual is saving, on average, $1,379.70 per month or 31.8% of their income, well above recommended levels
  2. Month-to-month spending is mostly consistent throughout the year. Aside from a large $8,000 home improvement charge in May, the only other spikes are in April and December, from which we can examine the data in Excel and draw a couple of conclusions.
  1. In April, there are additional charges in the Home Improvement category. This could be correlated with the large expense in May. We can confirm these charges as an expected expense with the individual once we present the analysis
  2. December spending can be attributed to holiday spending
  1. Discretionary spending is mostly acceptable, aside from a few points:
  1. Spending on Amazon is significant at $1,418.5 for the year
  2. A large amount of spending on a restaurant called American Tavern to the tune of $594.90 for the year

Overall, this individual’s spending is well within their budgetary constraints and I would consider them in a healthy situation with a monthly savings rate of almost 32%. If this individual wanted to raise the savings rate even more, I would identify Amazon (average spend of $118 per month), cell phone plan ($91 per month), and restaurants $136 per month) as places that they could possibly cut back on.
